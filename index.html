WeWatch

Mobile App Design

Overview

WeWatch combines all of your watchlists into one mobile app and makes choosing what to watch with others easier by letting you compare all watchlists across all streaming services. It also lets you connect with your friends by functioning as a social catalog.

User Research

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

We analyzed four direct competitors and one indirect competitor. Strengths of the competitors included simple and straightforward design, social functionality, ability to link to other accounts, and features like filters, lists, and ratings. Weaknesses of competitors included obtrusive ad content, lack of TV content, an inflexible rating system, and difficulty removing people from your network.

USER INTERVIEWS

We were interested in exploring how people make decisions on what to watch when they are in a group setting. We hypothesized that creating a TV/film social catalog application for dedicated viewers would centralize and socialize their entertainment consumption. We conducted six moderated, qualitative interviews with regular consumers of TV/film who have multiple streaming services and watch TV/film socially.

We found that most users place more importance on creating quality connections than the quality of the media they are consuming when watching socially. Most users did prefer watching TV/film with others as opposed to watching alone. However, we found a common pain point of the time investment it takes to come to a consensus on what to watch with others. We determined that dedicated viewers need a way to facilitate the group watching experience.

Definition

THE PROBLEM

The time investment it takes to decide what to watch in a group setting is a barrier to the interpersonal connections that it builds.

Ideation

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION

We decided to focus on the following features:

  • watchlist comparison
  • list creation and sharing
  • recommendations
  • activity sharing with friends
  • following popular users

Future Opportunities

GROUP COMPARISON

Streamlined comparison feature for 2+ people at once.

VIEWING TYPES + PREDICTIONS

Options to facilitate both remote and in-person shared viewing. Remote shared viewing requires an overlap of streaming services, while in-person does not. WeWatch could predict your watchlist for a streaming service you don't have based on your interests. Also if you have nothing in common!

FILTERING

Option to filter results given in the comparison page based on media type, genre, watch time, and more.

GAMIFICATION

The introduction of tiers, challenges, and badges serves as a reward system that reinforces the use of WeWatch.
